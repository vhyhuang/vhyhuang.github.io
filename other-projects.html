Doodlebug

Doodlebug is a startup focused on transitioning the aquaculture industry to more sustainable feed made from dried black soldier fly larvae (BSFL).

To make traditional feed, small fish are caught by wide net trawling which damages the ocean floor and risks overfishing. BSFL are almost 50% protein by weight and one female lays 500 eggs, therefore, growing them as feed is extremely cost efficient. Additionally, to feed the BSFL, we will take food waste from farmers, supermakets, and food wholesalers, which in turn promotes a more sustainable food chain in Canada.

By competing on sustainability, price, and customer satisfaction, Doodlebug has the potential to change the way Canadians grow their food.

For this project, I was responsible for branding, logo design, and promotional materials, as well as product research and market analysis. Our final deliverable was a live pitch that won the Founders' School Challenge out of 11 teams.
